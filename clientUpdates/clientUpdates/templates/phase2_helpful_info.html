Water Sources and Testing:

As defined by the settlements, a Water Source is:

  1. A groundwater well, with sampling done at the wellhead.
  2. A surface water treatment plant (supplied by one or more surface water sources or surface water combined with groundwater), with sampling done at the entry point into the treatment plant.
  3. Used, now or in the past, for drinking water. I.e., any Water Source that is currently active or inactive that was ever rused for drinking water
Impacted Water Sources:

An “Impacted Water Source” is an above-described Water Source that has a test result with any measurable concentration of any PFAS analyte, regardless of whether that source is above or below local or federal regulations. PFAS samples must be taken before any treatment that removes PFAS but can be after other non-PFAS treatments like chlorination. Groundwater samples taken from blended water locations (e.g. an entry point into a distribution system where two groundwater wells are combined) are not eligible. The date by which these settlements determine eligibility of a PFAS test is the Sampling Date, not analysis date or lab report date.

Unimpacted Water Sources:

The settlements require that all non-detect sources, and sources that have never been tested, be tested for all 29 PFAS analytes under EPA methods 533 and 537.1 to comply with Baseline Testing requirements. By submitting baseline-compliant non-detect sources with supporting lab reports, such sources are eligible to submit Supplemental Fund claims one or more of those sources exceed a State or Federal MCL before the end of 2030. We strongly advise testing any such sources to ensure potential eligibility for the settlement process. If you are unable or do not want to test a source for any reason, please let EH&E know.


Claim Documentation

Document Submission - Lab Reports:
For PFAS contamination, the Claims Administrator will require the lab reports with chain-of-custody records reflecting the highest PFAS values for each Water Source of all time, or for sources without detections, the lab reports that establish Baseline Testing (see the "Unimpacted Water Sources" section above for more details). Note: If ongoing sampling results in higher PFAS values, please provide the lab reports for those higher detections. The results submitted do not need to be representative of the current levels of PFAS, just the historically highest levels.

The necessary PFAS lab reports for each of your Water Sources can be provided by completing a "Source Form", located within the landing page.

Document Submission - Flow Rates:
For maximum flow rate, this documentation will consist of one of the following:

  1. The design specifications for each source;
  2. A report of a pump test for the well or surface water intake; or,
  3. A system-wide file that lists the capacity of each source.

Note: we may submit the highest maximum flow rate that’s been documented, even if it is not representative of the current capacity or use. If you do not have any of the above, EH&E can assist you in determining the best way to calculate flow rate based on historical production.

For annual flow rates, you will likely have records of annual (or monthly) production on a source-by-source basis, typically maintained in an Excel file or other log, often recorded in gallons or acre feet. If not, or if you have never kept track of that on a well-by-well basis, please let us know so we can assist you in estimating your historical production.

The necessary maximum flow rate and annual flow rate documentation for each of your Water Sources can be provided by completing a "Source Form", located within the landing page.
